First of all, what is cider? In the USA and also in Canada, ‚Äòcider‚Äô is used to describe a cloudy but unfermented (non-alcoholic) apple juice, often from an orchard stand or farmer‚Äôs market. The term ‚Äòhard cider‚Äô is used to denote that this cider has been fermented, and now contains alcohol. To differentiate between hard cider and cider, sometimes you will see the terms ‚Äúsweet cider‚Äù used. In most of the rest of the world, including the UK and Europe, just saying plain ‚Äòcider‚Äô means alcoholic hard cider. Just remember, after you add the yeast, it is no longer sweet cider, so I might just call it ‚Äúcider‚Äù by that stage in the process out of habit.

## Is making my own hard cider legal?

YES! In the U.S., as long as you _don‚Äôt sell it_, every adult in your home may brew up to 100 gallons of wine, beer or cider per year. More than that, and you need a liquor license‚Ä¶ and likely a 12-Step Program.
